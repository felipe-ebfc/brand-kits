04 ‚Äî Voice & Tone

Voice & Tone

Educational, authoritative, practical, and evidence-based. We cite data. We build credibility one fact at a time.

‚úì Do This
  • Lead with data ‚Äî cite research and project outcomes
  • Use construction-native language (foreman, RFI, submittal)
  • Be direct and prescriptive: "Run 2-week sprints, not 6-month phases"
  • Acknowledge the real problems of the field first
  • Celebrate wins with specifics ‚Äî "Saved 18 days on concrete pour"
  • Frame Scrum as practical, not theoretical
‚úï Avoid This
  • Generic agile talk with no construction context
  • Jargon-heavy tech-bro language
  • Vague promises without evidence ("transforms your team!")
  • Dismissing traditional construction methods outright
  • Overly casual or jokey tone on serious project failure data
  • Hiding uncertainty ‚Äî be honest about what Scrum can't fix
Stat Lead
"75% of construction projects finish late. Scrum closes that gap ‚Äî sprint by sprint."
Challenge/Solution
"Your schedule isn't broken. Your planning method is. Here's how to fix it."
Field-Forward
"Scrum isn't software. It's a rhythm. And the best jobsites already run on it."
Transformation
"From RFI bottlenecks to daily standups that actually solve them. One sprint at a time."
