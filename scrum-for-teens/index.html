05 — Voice & Tone

How We Sound

EmpoweringSpeaks directly to teens as capable, not clueless. "You can do this."
ApproachableWarm, friendly, never jargon-heavy. No corporate speak.
CredibleGrounded in real results — classroom wins, competition victories.
Teen-FriendlyUses analogies teens relate to (sports, gaming, Shakespeare). No condescension.

Core principle: We speak directly to teens — "you," "your," not "students" or "users." Short paragraphs (2–3 sentences). Active voice. Encouragement over instruction. Real stories: Noah's journey, classroom wins, competition results.

06 — Usage Guidelines

Do's and Don'ts

✓ Do
  • Address readers as "you" — never "students" or "users"
  • Use warm browns and beige as primary palette — this is a friendly brand
  • Keep "Scrum" in ink/white and "for Teens" in warm brown accent
  • Lead with real outcomes: competition wins, classroom success stories
  • Use sports/gaming/Shakespeare analogies — they land with teen readers
  • Reference Noah's authorship journey — teen-to-teen voice matters
  • Keep paragraphs 2–3 sentences max on screen
✕ Don't
  • Use corporate jargon: "leverage," "synergy," "optimization"
  • Condescend or talk down to teen readers
  • Separate "Scrum" from "for Teens" in the wordmark — one unit
  • Use blue, green, or other brand colors from EBFC AI — this is a separate brand
  • Lead with abstract benefits — ground in real, specific examples
  • Use hard-hat or stock construction photos — this is education, not jobsites
  • Oversell — be honest about what Scrum can and can't do for teens
